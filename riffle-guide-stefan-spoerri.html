About Stefan

Thirty years of Delaware River life

Stefan Spoerri first encountered the Upper Delaware River more than thirty years ago and was immediately captivated by the diversity of life in its crystal-clear waters. He made the river valley his home shortly after. Since then, he has spent virtually every available moment on the water — observing hatches, studying fish behavior, and developing an intimate understanding of how the Delaware changes through every season.

After more than fifteen years as a professional fishing guide, Stefan has logged thousands of river trips and tens of thousands of miles rowing clients through the finest trout water in the East. His drift boat — a custom-built 2014 ClackaCraft 16' Low Profile — is perfectly matched to the Delaware's character: efficient in its broad flat pools, agile in its technical riffles.

In his time away from the water, Stefan ties the flies he and his clients fish. The patterns are developed specifically for Delaware River conditions — emerging mayflies, spent spinners, and nymph imitations built from years of watching what the river's fish actually eat. Every client gets a selection before they start fishing.

The Upper Delaware

The best dry-fly water east of the Rockies

Why guides and anglers choose the Delaware
The Upper Delaware system produces hatches of a size and variety that rivals anything in the American West. Wild brown and rainbow trout — genuinely wild, not stocked — feed selectively on emerging mayflies, caddis, and stoneflies throughout the season. The fish are educated and demanding. Fooling a Delaware brown on a dry fly is a serious accomplishment. Stefan has guided this water so thoroughly that he knows individual pools, specific lies, and the precise timing of every major hatch.
West Branch Delaware
Tailwater below Cannonsville. Coldest, most consistent. Trophy brown trout all season. Stefan's primary water.
East Branch Delaware
Warmer, more varied. Wild trout early season, excellent bass and shad summer. Scenic, uncrowded water.
Main Stem Delaware
Big water where both branches meet at Hancock. Trout, smallmouth bass, and American shad. Drift boat essential.
