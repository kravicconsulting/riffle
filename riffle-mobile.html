The Birthplace

Fly fishing was born here.

The Catskill Mountains are where American fly fishing began. The Beaverkill, the Willowemoc, the Delaware — these rivers shaped everything. Theodore Gordon first cast a dry fly here in the 1880s.

The dry fly was born on Catskill water, and it remains the truest test of a fly angler's skill. — Theodore Gordon, Father of American Dry Fly Fishing

Fly Fishing Techniques

Every Discipline

Find a guide matched to how you fish — or how you want to learn.

Dry Fly

The most iconic form — presenting flies on the surface to rising fish. The heartbeat of Catskill tradition.

620 guides
Nymphing

Sub-surface fishing with weighted flies. Deadly year-round and the best way for beginners to start.

780 guides
Streamer Fishing

Large baitfish-imitating flies stripped aggressively. Produces the biggest fish of the season.

410 guides
Saltwater Fly

Sight-casting to bonefish, permit, and tarpon on tropical flats. The ultimate fly fishing challenge.

265 guides
Spey Casting

Two-handed casting for big salmon and steelhead rivers. Sweeping and efficient on large water.

190 guides
Euro Nymphing

High-stick, tight-line technique from European competition fishing. Deadly on technical streams.
