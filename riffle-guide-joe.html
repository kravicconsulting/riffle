About Joe

Where the sport began — fished by someone who lives it

Joe grew up in northwestern Pennsylvania fishing stocked trout with his grandfather on opening day. He was introduced to fly fishing in his late teens and eventually built a life in Sullivan County, New York — the place where American fly fishing took root. He has spent years on the Willowemoc, Beaverkill, Neversink, and all branches of the Delaware, developing a deep obsession with technical dry fly fishing to selective trout.

What sets Joe apart is his philosophy. He doesn't guide the way most guides guide. His days are designed as learning experiences — he wants anglers to leave able to read water they've never seen, understand what's hatching, and fish with real confidence. He is a patient, gifted teacher with a long list of clients who started as beginners and now plan annual trips back.

Joe ties all his own flies, matched specifically to the insects of these Catskill rivers. Every client gets a set for the day. He also provides video and photography of your trip — a genuine differentiator you won't find anywhere else in the region.

Trout Unlimited Lifetime MemberActive in Catskills conservation — streambank restoration, water quality monitoring, and TU youth programs.
Photo & video included on every tripJoe captures footage and stills throughout your day and sends a curated edit after. No extra cost — it's part of how he guides.
