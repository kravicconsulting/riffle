About Mike
A lifetime on Catskill water

Mike Walsh grew up a mile from the Beaverkill. He was casting a fly rod before he was ten years old, taught by his grandfather on the same runs he now guides for clients from around the world. He has spent more than two decades learning every riffle, pool, seam, and eddy on the Beaverkill, Willowemoc, and upper Delaware river system.

He is known for his patience with beginners and his ability to put experienced anglers into fish that other guides walk past. He does not take every client who asks — he is selective about who he guides because he cares about the experience on both sides of the rod.

Mike is an Orvis-Endorsed guide and a licensed New York State guide. He holds a first aid certification and carries full liability insurance on every trip.

Specialties
What Mike does best
Dry Fly Fishing
Reading hatches and presenting emergers and dries to rising fish. Mike lives for the evening rise on the Beaverkill.
Euro Nymphing
Tight-line technique for technical runs and pockets. Highly effective year-round when fish aren't rising.
Streamer Fishing
Big flies, big fish. Mike knows the trophy holding lies on the Delaware's main stem and West Branch.
Casting Instruction
Works with all levels. Certified casting instructor — ideal for beginners and those wanting to break bad habits.
