About Irwin

Three generations of Catskill water

Irwin Mallory is a third-generation Catskill fly fisherman. His grandfather fished the Batavia Kill in the Northern Catskills. His father taught him to cast. Irwin began stalking trout on the same rivers his family had fished for decades before he was old enough to drive himself to the water. That was more than forty years ago.

His resume reads like a map of the best fly water in New York state. The Esopus, Beaverkill, Willowemoc, and Schoharie — he knows each one the way most people know their own neighborhood. Not in a general way, but specifically: which pool holds fish in a July low-water year, which run produces the first Hendricksons in April, where the big browns hold in October when the water drops cold and clear.

Irwin guides beginners and experts with equal patience. His focus is on sharing knowledge — he wants every client to understand the water they're fishing, not just catch fish on it. He is one of the most experienced wade guides in the Catskills region.

"The Catskills are where American fly fishing was born. I was lucky enough to be born into a family that already loved these rivers. I've been trying to share that love ever since."
— Irwin Mallory, third-generation Catskill guide

The Mallory Family's Catskill Waters

Three generations on the same rivers

Generation 1
Batavia Kill
Irwin's grandfather fished the Batavia Kill and Schoharie Creek in the Northern Catskills — high, cold freestone streams with native brook trout. This is where the family's relationship with Catskill water began.
Generation 2
Beaverkill
Irwin's father moved the family's fishing focus to the Beaverkill and Willowemoc — the heart of the Catskill dry fly tradition. He taught Irwin to cast on these rivers before Irwin was ten years old.
Generation 3
All four rivers
Irwin extended the family range to cover the Esopus, Schoharie, Beaverkill, and Willowemoc. More than four decades of guiding, combined with his inherited knowledge of these waters, makes him among the deepest wells of Catskill angling expertise alive today.
